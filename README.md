## Project Overview
The objective of this analysis is to explore the relationship between social media usage and mental health indicators across various demographics in Southeast Asia. The analysis aims to identify patterns and correlations between factors such as daily social media usage, self-confidence, social anxiety levels, and experiences of cyberbullying.

Through this exploration, the project seeks to provide insights that can inform product recommendations for mental health apps, as well as methods for predicting the impact of social media on mental well-being.


## Questions
How does daily social media usage vary across different age groups and genders?
This question will guide the exploration of usage patterns among various demographics.
What is the correlation between social media usage and self-confidence levels?
Investigating this relationship may highlight potential implications for mental health.
How does peer comparison frequency affect social anxiety levels?
This question aims to uncover insights into social behaviors that impact mental health.
What is the prevalence of cyberbullying experiences among different demographic groups?
This analysis will help identify vulnerable populations affected by cyberbullying.
Can we identify clusters of users based on their social media usage and mental health indicators?
This will explore whether distinct user groups can be formed based on their responses.
